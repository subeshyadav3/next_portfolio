## The Story

Once upon a time, in a small village nestled at the foot of green hills, there lived a poor but honest woodcutter named Ram. Every morning, Ram would take his axe and walk to the forest to cut wood, which he would then sell in the village market to earn a modest living for himself and his family.

Despite his poverty, Ram was known throughout the village for his honesty. He never cheated anyone, always gave the correct measure of wood, and was respected by everyone who knew him.

<Quote author="Traditional Nepali Saying">
ईमानदारी सबैभन्दा ठूलो धन हो — Honesty is the greatest wealth.
</Quote>

Ram lived by this principle every single day.

One fateful day, while cutting wood near a river, Ram's axe slipped from his hands and fell into the deep water. The axe sank instantly and disappeared into the dark depths. This was a disaster for Ram. His axe was his only tool and his sole means of earning a living. Without it, he could not cut wood or support his family. He sat by the riverbank and wept in despair.

## The Divine Test

As Ram sat there crying, the river goddess appeared before him. She was a radiant figure dressed in flowing white, and her voice was gentle like the flowing water. She decided to test Ram's honesty. First, she held up a beautiful golden axe and asked, "Is this golden axe yours?"

Ram looked at the golden axe and shook his head. "No, goddess. My axe is made of iron."

Then the goddess showed a shining silver axe. "Then surely this one is yours?"

Again, Ram replied honestly, "No, goddess. That is not mine either. My axe is a simple iron one."

Finally, the goddess showed his old, worn iron axe. Ram's face lit up. "Yes!" he exclaimed happily. "That is my axe!"

Many people in Ram's situation might have claimed the golden or silver axe. He was poor, and no one would have known if he had lied. But his conscience would not allow it. He chose honesty over wealth, even though it meant remaining poor.

## The Reward

The river goddess was deeply pleased with Ram's honesty. "You have passed my test," she said. "You could have easily claimed the golden or silver axe, but you chose truth over greed. Your honesty has touched my heart. I will reward you with all three axes — the golden, the silver, and your own iron axe."

Ram returned to his village with the three axes. He sold the golden and silver axes for a good price and used the money to build a comfortable home for his family, buy better tools for his work, and help his neighbors who were also struggling. He continued to work as a woodcutter, but now he had enough to live comfortably and to share with others.

## The Moral

The story of the honest woodcutter teaches us lessons that are as relevant today as they were centuries ago. Honesty is always rewarded, even when it seems like no one is watching. Greed leads to loss — if Ram had lied out of greed, he would have lost everything. Character matters more than wealth. Ram's honesty was far more valuable than any golden axe could ever be. And help comes to those who deserve it. The goddess appeared because Ram was genuinely honest, not because he was seeking a reward.

Integrity means doing the right thing even when no one is watching. Ram demonstrated this perfectly by refusing the golden and silver axes even though he was poor and could have easily gotten away with the lie. His story reminds us that a person's character is built through small choices made every day.

## Discussion Questions

You might wonder why the river goddess tested Ram instead of simply returning his axe. The answer is that she wanted to see if Ram was truly honest or only honest when it was convenient. The test revealed the true nature of his character, and that is what mattered.

What lesson can we take from this story into our daily lives? We face similar choices every day — returning extra change a shopkeeper gave us by mistake, admitting when we have made an error, telling the truth when a lie would be easier. Each choice builds our character, one decision at a time.

Is it always good to be honest even when it hurts? Yes, because honesty builds trust and reputation. While telling the truth may sometimes be difficult in the short term, honesty always pays off in the long run. And what would have happened if Ram had lied? In some versions of the story, the goddess would have vanished with all the axes, and Ram would have lost even his own. Dishonesty ultimately leads to losing everything.

## The Story in Nepali Context

This story holds a special place in Nepali culture, where honesty is considered one of the highest virtues. The concept of having a clean heart is deeply valued, and elders often tell stories like these to teach children the importance of truthfulness. In today's world, honesty is still the best policy. Whether in school exams, business dealings, or personal relationships, honest people earn trust and respect. The honest woodcutter's story reminds us that integrity is never outdated.

## Conclusion

The honest woodcutter is a timeless tale that has been told for generations across cultures. In Nepal, grandparents share this story to teach children that honesty is its own reward. As you go through life, remember Ram's example. Choose honesty, even when no one is watching, because your character is the most valuable thing you possess. It is worth more than all the gold and silver in the world.
